How to Read the Diagrams

The simple diagrams below show where players line up before the snap. They are drawn from the offense's point of view, moving left to right across the field.

Key: QB = Quarterback, RB = Running Back, FB = Fullback, WR = Wide Receiver, TE = Tight End, OL = Offensive Line, DL = Defensive Line, LB = Linebacker, CB = Cornerback, S = Safety.

Offensive Formations (Text Diagrams)

I-Formation (Traditional, Run-Heavy)

The offense lines up under center with two backs directly behind the quarterback. This keeps defenders guessing which back will get the ball and is great for downhill running. 

 WR        LT LG  C  RG RT        TE        WR
                    QB
                    FB RB

Shotgun Spread (Pass-First Look)

In shotgun the quarterback stands a few yards behind the center, which gives more time to read the defense. A spread look uses multiple wide receivers to stretch the defense horizontally. 

   WR        WR        WR        WR

        LT LG  C  RG RT     TE

                 QB    RB

Trips Right (Three Receivers to One Side)

Trips puts three receivers on the same side of the field. This can overload a zone coverage and create natural picks and rub routes in man coverage. 

   WR  WR  WR                    WR

        LT LG  C  RG RT      TE

                 QB    RB

Defensive Fronts (Text Diagrams)

4–3 Defense

The 4–3 uses four down linemen and three linebackers. It is balanced against both the run and the pass. 

          CB           S          S           CB


        DE    DT    DT    DE
             OLB   MLB   OLB

Nickel Defense (5 Defensive Backs)

A nickel package removes one linebacker and adds an extra defensive back to better match up with three- or four-wide-receiver sets. 

      CB       S       S       CB       NB


        DE    DT    DT    DE
              MLB      OLB

Dime Defense (6 Defensive Backs)

Dime is an even lighter personnel package, usually used on obvious passing downs. The defense sacrifices size in the box to get more speed and coverage on the field.

        DE    DT    DT    DE
                MLB
