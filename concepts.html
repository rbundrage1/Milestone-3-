Key Concepts

These core ideas explain how football actually works. Before learning formations or positions, you should understand downs, yardage, drives, scoring, and basic offensive and defensive strategy.

Downs & Yardage (The Heart of Football)

Every possession revolves around a simple goal: gain 10 yards in 4 downs. If the offense reaches 10 yards, they earn a first down and get a new set of four downs.

  • 1st Down: Start of a series; play calls are flexible.
  • 2nd Down: Adjust based on how many yards are left.
  • 3rd Down: Higher pressure; teams often pass more.
  • 4th Down: Usually a punt or field goal, unless the team “goes for it.”

Coaches talk about down-and-distance to describe the situation, like “3rd and 4” or “2nd and 10.”

Field Structure

A football field is 100 yards long with a 10-yard end zone at each end. Numbers on the field show each 10-yard increment.

  • End Zones: Where touchdowns are scored.
  • Hash Marks: The exact left/right spot where each play begins.
  • Sidelines: Stepping out of bounds stops the game clock and the play.

Basic Offensive Structure

Offenses blend run plays, pass plays, and option plays. The quarterback (QB) reads the defense to decide where the ball should go.

  • Run Play: QB hands the ball to a running back (RB).
  • Pass Play: QB throws to a wide receiver (WR) or tight end (TE).
  • Run-Pass Option (RPO): QB chooses to hand off or throw after the snap.

Basic Defensive Structure

Defenses try to stop the offense by balancing coverage and pressure.

  • Man Coverage: Each defender guards a specific receiver.
  • Zone Coverage: Defenders cover areas of the field.
  • Blitz: Extra players rush the quarterback to force quick decisions.
