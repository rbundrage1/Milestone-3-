Rules & Scoring

This page explains the basic rules, scoring system, timing, penalties, and common game situations in American football. Understanding these ideas makes it much easier to follow a live game.

Basic Objective

The goal of football is to score more points than the other team. The offense tries to move the ball down the field and score, while the defense tries to stop them, force a turnover, or make them punt.

Players on the Field

Each team has 11 players on the field at a time. One team plays offense, the other plays defense, and special teams come on for kicks, punts, and returns.

Downs & Distance

Football is built around the down system. The offense gets four downs to gain 10 yards. If they gain 10 or more yards, they earn a new first down.

  • 1st Down: The start of a new set of plays.
  • 2nd Down: The offense adjusts based on yards gained or lost.
  • 3rd Down: A key pressure down because the offense wants to keep the drive alive.
  • 4th Down: The team usually punts, kicks a field goal, or attempts to gain the remaining yards.

Example: If the announcer says “3rd and 4”, that means it is third down and the offense needs four yards for a new first down.

Scoring System

Scoring Play Points What It Means
Touchdown 6 The ball is carried or caught in the opponent’s end zone.
Extra Point Kick 1 After a touchdown, the team kicks the ball through the uprights.
Two-Point Conversion 2 After a touchdown, the team runs or passes the ball into the end zone.
Field Goal 3 The kicker kicks the ball through the uprights during a drive.
Safety 2 The defense tackles the offense in its own end zone.

Game Clock

A football game is divided into four quarters. In the NFL and college football, each quarter is 15 minutes long. The clock does not run continuously because it can stop after certain plays.

  • The clock may stop after incomplete passes.
  • The clock may stop when a player goes out of bounds.
  • Teams can call timeouts to stop the clock.
  • The clock stops for penalties, injuries, reviews, and changes of possession.

Common Penalties

Penalty Common Yardage Explanation
False Start 5 yards An offensive player moves before the ball is snapped.
Offside 5 yards A defensive player crosses the line of scrimmage too early.
Holding Usually 10 yards A player illegally grabs or restricts an opponent.
Pass Interference Varies A player illegally prevents an opponent from catching a pass.
Delay of Game 5 yards The offense does not snap the ball before the play clock expires.

Turnovers

A turnover happens when the offense loses possession of the ball. Turnovers are important because they can quickly change momentum.

  • Interception: A defensive player catches a pass thrown by the quarterback.
  • Fumble: A player loses control of the ball before being ruled down.
  • Turnover on Downs: The offense fails to gain enough yards on fourth down.

Special Teams

Special teams are used for kicking plays. These plays can affect field position and scoring.

  • Kickoff: Starts each half and follows most scoring plays.
  • Punt: Usually happens on fourth down when the offense gives up possession.
  • Field Goal Attempt: A kick for three points.
  • Extra Point Attempt: A kick after a touchdown for one point.

Simple Game Situation Example

Imagine the offense has the ball at midfield. It is 2nd and 6. That means the offense has already used first down and still needs six more yards to earn a new set of downs. The coach might call a short pass, a run play, or a play-action pass depending on the defense.
